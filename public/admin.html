What is Cronbach's Alpha ($\alpha$)? It is a measure of internal consistency reliability. For Likert-scale indexes used in theses (e.g., Section C and Section D), it proves that your questions consistently measure the exact same latent psychological variable. An $\alpha \ge 0.70$ is the standard academic threshold for acceptable reliability.

Demographic Crosstabulations & Chi-Square Significance ($\chi^2$)

Cross-tabulations reveal relationships between demographic variables (independent) and empirical AI detection accuracy (dependent). The Chi-Square Test of Independence computes a p-value indicating whether these differences are statistically significant or occurred by random chance. A $p < 0.05$ indicates a strong scholarly relationship!
